title: "Client portal"
description: "Give your customers real-time order visibility and delivery photo access through the client portal."
---

The client portal gives customers a dedicated, read-only view of their orders. Clients can track their current order, review past orders, and view photographic delivery evidence — all without being able to modify any data.

## How clients get access

Clients do not self-register. An administrator creates a **Cliente** role account on their behalf and shares the credentials.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Admin creates the client account">
    An administrator navigates to **Users → New User**, enters the customer's name and email, sets a password, and assigns the **Cliente** role. Only administrators can perform this step.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Client receives credentials">
    The administrator shares the login credentials with the customer out of band (email, phone, etc.).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Client logs in">
    The customer navigates to the Halcon login page and signs in with their credentials. After authentication, the application detects the **Cliente** role and redirects to `/cliente/dashboard`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
If a client tries to access any other section of the application — such as `/admin/dashboard` or `/orders` — the role middleware will block the request. Clients only have access to the client dashboard route.
</Note>

## The client dashboard

After login, clients land on `/cliente/dashboard`. The dashboard is a clean, read-only view showing:

- A welcome message with the client's name
- Their active order and its current status
- Historical orders they have previously received
- Photographic evidence for any order in **Delivered** status

## Tracking an order

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to the dashboard">
    After logging in, the client is automatically taken to `/cliente/dashboard`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="View your orders">
    The dashboard lists all your current and past orders automatically, sorted from newest to oldest. No search is required — all orders linked to your account appear here.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the order status">
    Each order card displays the current status. Use the table below to understand what each status means.
  </Step>
  <Step title="View delivery evidence">
    If the order status is **Delivered**, a photo thumbnail appears below the order details. Click the thumbnail to view the full-size delivery photo uploaded by the Route team.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Order status meanings for customers

| Database value | What it means to the customer |
|---|---|
| `pending` | Your order has been registered and is being prepared for dispatch. |
| `in_route` | Your order has been dispatched and is on its way to the delivery address. |
| `delivered` | Your order has been delivered. Photographic evidence is available below. |

## Viewing photographic evidence

Delivery photos become visible as soon as the Route team marks an order as **Delivered** and uploads the evidence image.

The photo is displayed as a thumbnail on the order card. It is served directly from Halcon's storage:

```
/storage/evidences/<filename>
```

<Note>
Evidence photos are only visible when the order status is `delivered`. If the Route team has not yet uploaded a photo, the image area will not appear even if the status is Delivered.
</Note>

## Viewing historical orders

The client dashboard shows both the current active order and all past orders associated with the client's account. Orders are listed from newest to oldest.

Historical orders display the same information as the active order — status, description, and delivery evidence — giving clients a complete record of all their previous deliveries.

## What clients cannot do

<Note>
The client role is strictly read-only. Clients cannot create, edit, update the status of, or delete any order.
</Note>

Specifically, clients have no access to:

- The order creation form
- Status update controls
- The evidence upload input
- User management pages
- Any other department dashboard (`/admin`, `/ventas`, `/almacen`, `/ruta`, `/compras`)

All of these routes are protected by role middleware. Attempting to access them while authenticated as a **Cliente** will result in an authorization error.
